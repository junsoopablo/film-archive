Bolex (Paillard)

Switzerland · 1923–1980s

Founded by an 'ambitious, mysterious self-made man'

In 1923, Jaques Bogopolsky — described as 'an ambitious, mysterious self-made man' — founded Compagnie Bol S.A in Switzerland. The company was later acquired by precision instrument maker Paillard.

The Paillard-Bolex H16 camera became legendary. In projectors, the 1949 M8 series sold 158,000 units. The innovative 1968 Multimatic featured automatic cartridge-based film loading.

Bolex projector production eventually passed to Austria's Eumig, which manufactured roughly 200,000 sound and 120,000 silent projectors under the Bolex name. The era ended with Eumig's bankruptcy.
