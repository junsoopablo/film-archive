Heurtier

France · Saint-Étienne · 1930–1981

A 13-year-old dropout who became an inventor with 65 patents

Antoine Heurtier was a church choir singer and theater performer at 12, a school dropout at 13. After working at 24 different factories in Saint-Étienne, the self-taught mechanic started his own business at 28.

The turning point came from his father: 'Shall I buy a little projector for the grandchildren?' But the purchased projector broke after just 8 days, and the salesman said: 'I can't give you another — they're all the same low quality.' That moment, Heurtier decided to build his own.

His 'Trifilm' was revolutionary — one projector that could play 9.5mm, 16mm, and 8mm film. Orders arrived within 8 days. 'It was the finest device on the world market,' he said passionately.

In 1981, his patents expired and 'Japanese companies immediately copied everything at 40% cheaper prices.' The company closed, but Swiss Bolex Paillard had purchased Heurtier's patents — recognition that the self-taught man without credentials had earned his place.
