Meopta

Czechoslovakia · Přerov · 1933–present

Czech optical excellence born in a 3×5 meter workshop

In 1930, Jindrich Suchanek began making school laboratory equipment in a tiny 3×5 meter workshop. He soon moved to 16mm projectors — his apartment-made machines produced brighter images than expensive foreign competitors.

By 1935, with 17 employees in a textile factory, he produced the ALMO — remarkably similar to Emel and Bell & Howell projectors. Nationalized in 1948 as Meopta, the company produced everything from 35mm theater projectors to amateur 8mm models.

Notably, Meopta never produced a single amateur sound projector — a reflection of socialist Czechoslovakia's industrial priorities.

Today, Meopta occupies 135,000m² with 2,400 employees, known for military optics and riflescopes.
